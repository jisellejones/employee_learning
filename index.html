Considerations
Employee Participation

Overall, the participants represented approximately 4% of Company Employees.

Roughly 75% of participants were in career grade C2, D, and E. Of these participants, ​about 90% in each category were white. The other 10% of candidates identified as Black, Asian, Latino/Hispanic, Prefer not to say, and Other. Approximately half of participants identified as male. The majority of the other half of participants identified as female, while around 3% - 4% identified as other or preferred not to say. Most participants were from the US, the UK, and Canada. 


  • QUESTIONS
  • Is it possible to collaborate with Employee Resource Groups to increase diversity of participants across ethnicities, career grades, and gender identities?

  • In future surveys, is it possible to include other gender identifications (such as: transgender, non-binary​, queer)?

  • Note for consideration: In future analyses, consider including gender/ethnicity data when looking at program impact.

Considerations
Program Impacts

The McKinsey Leadership programs seem to outshine the other three when considering only NPS scores; however, it is necessary to note that there were only 13 participants in the McKinsey Black Leadership program and 22 in the McKinsey Asian Leadership Program. There were 348 participants in iLead, 801 in iManage, and 105 in iLead. The smaller groups may have contributed to the higher ratings. 

QUESTIONS FOR FUTURE ANALYSIS
What is the cost of each program? Is a smaller program actually more cost effective? How can this be measured?

What can we learn from the comments offered about each program? Is there a way to enhance the participant experience in iLead, iManage, and Lead to Succeed? What did work in these programs? Why were the McKinsey programs so impactful for participants?

What is the impact of the program on an employee in the months following? How does the employee's review status change? Does an employee move to a leadership position across units? Does an employee make a step up (example: from a C2 to a D)?

If an employee were to leave the company for a leadership role in another company, is this considered a "success"? How can the Company retain diverse leaders that the company has invested in?

Would including natural language processing to determine participant sentiment across programs offer insight into the effectiveness or ineffectiveness of programs?
